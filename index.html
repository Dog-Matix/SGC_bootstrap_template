Case Studies

Local Authority

0-19yrs Healthy Weight Management Programme

April 2016 – December 2016

The aim was to commission a service provider to design and deliver an accessible tier 2/3 weight management service which will form part of the authority’s emerging integrated weight management pathway. The aim of this service will be to reduce the number of overweight and obese children, young people and families (CYP&F). Although funded by the local authority, collaboration with schools, Community Health and local NHS services was essential for success.

Value £1.5m

Local Authority – Public Health

Substance Misuse Service

May 2015 – April 2016

A range of services provided to service a growing cohort of Substance Misuse users across Re-habilitation, Treatment, Education & Training and support that had seen the budget reduced by £10m and needed to achieve further efficiencies. Guiding the commissioners through a huge stakeholder engagement and consultation to alleviate fears and build a communication channel. It was widely believed a legal challenge to the process would be made due to the assumed damage that would be made on local NHS services. A sucessful and fair procurement resulted in service efficiencies, a saving of 0.5m and only two providers in place of twelve. The implementaion process took 3 months and was very complex to to the number of TUPE implications.

Value £37.5m

Local Authority – Adult Social Care

Alternate Service Delivery Models

September 2014 – March 2015

Working with the Transformation board, I with a number of colleagues discussed and researched a number of alternative service delivery models for the provision of care. Firstly all services within scope needed to be identified and the full costs of providing the service in-house to be measured against an alternative.

There are a variety of legal “vehicles” that can be used to set up the desired form of company. These “vehicles” are:

  • Company limited by shares
  • Company limited by guarantee
  • Industrial and Provident Society (Community benefit)
  • Industrial and Provident Society (Co-operative)
  • Limited Liability Partnership
  • Charitable Incorporated Organisation

The data analysis and consultation was immense and still on-going after my assignment was completed.

Local Authority – Adult Social Care

Community Equipment

May 2014 – December 2014

This was collaboration between two neighbouring local authorities to provide Community Equipment to Service Users and their Carers to promote and support independent living. The data analysis was huge and very complicated, some items of equipment not having been returned and or calibrated. Items included:

  • Home nursing equipment such as beds, mattresses and commodes
  • Equipment for daily living such as shower chairs, raised toilet seats
  • Moving and handling equipment such as hoists and slide sheets
  • Pressure relieving equipment such as cushions and mattresses
  • Minor Adaptations such as grab rails, access rails, fixed floor toilet frames.
  • Assessment equipment for demonstration and assessment purposes
  • Short term wheelchairs and accessories for both adults and children
  • Paediatric equipment

Value £21.6m
